I'm an undergraduate researcher working on autonomous driving perception, with a background in Formula Student vehicle engineering.

My research focuses on how perception systems behave in the real world, beyond clean benchmark conditions. I work on camera viewpoint changes, calibration errors, sensor synchronization, and real-vehicle deployment, with a particular interest in robust BEV perception and LiDAR-based reasoning.

Before moving into perception research, I led mechanical analysis for Formula Student vehicles from 2020 to 2024, working on monocoque chassis design, structural and thermal analysis, and component optimization. That experience shaped how I approach research today: a perception model is not just an algorithm, but part of a full vehicle system shaped by geometry, hardware, timing, and deployment limits.

## Research

### Robust BEV Perception

Multi-camera calibration and multi-view BEV semantic segmentation. Implemented and trained CVT-based models, analyzing how viewpoint changes and calibration errors affect performance using real-world data and CARLA simulation. Awarded Grand Prize (graduation thesis & poster).

### Real-Vehicle Perception (T-CAR Project)

Camera perception for a real-vehicle autonomous driving platform, including camera-LiDAR calibration, hardware synchronization, and StreamPETR deployment for 3D object detection with TensorRT optimization.

### Driver Behavior Analysis

Built a real-vehicle data acquisition environment (CAN, RT2000, PC, eCon Systems cameras) and proposed a multiple-instance time-series architecture for weakly supervised driver behavior classification.

### Formula Student Driverless

Team Lead for the 1st Korean Formula Student Driverless Competition (LiDAR-based perception). 1st place winner. Currently preparing the next real-vehicle driverless project.

### Ongoing

Real-vehicle data acquisition, LiDAR-camera unsupervised 3D object detection, LiDAR LLM for ground reasoning.
